# Native Playback Validation Suite (NPVS) — Specifications

| Req ID | Test Scope |
|:-------|:--------------------------------------|
| `NPVS‑REQ‑001` | SHALL manage the full playback pipeline lifecycle — initialization, state transitions to the PLAYING state, graceful shutdown releasing all resources without leaks, and pipeline reinitialization by playing the same asset twice sequentially without errors between playbacks, using the AAC audio codec and the H.264 video codec delivered via DASH streams and HLS streams. |
| `NPVS‑REQ‑002` | SHALL decode and render video playback at 1x rate with continuous frame rendering and no playback errors across all supported video codecs — H.264 (MP4 container, MOV container, DASH streams, HLS streams, and DASH content with a Dolby Digital audio track), HEVC/H.265 (including HEVC in the MKV container), VP9 (including VP9 HDR), AV1, and MPEG. |
| `NPVS‑REQ‑003` | SHALL decode and render audio-only playback to completion at 1x rate with continuous audio frame rendering across the supported audio codecs — AAC and WAV. |
| `NPVS‑REQ‑004` | SHALL play 4K video for the H.264, HEVC (MKV container), VP9, and AV1 codecs and via DASH and HLS adaptive streams, correctly reporting 4K video dimensions and rendering frames at 1x rate without decoding failures or drops. |
| `NPVS‑REQ‑005` | MAY play video at 360p and 480p resolutions (H.264), correctly reporting the rendered video dimensions at each resolution without decoding failures. |
| `NPVS‑REQ‑006` | SHALL play video at 720p and 1080p resolutions across the applicable codecs (H.264 and AV1), correctly reporting the rendered video dimensions at each resolution without decoding failures. |
| `NPVS‑REQ‑007` | SHALL correctly detect and preserve both 8-bit and 10-bit source color depth throughout playback, reporting the color-depth metadata via the platform decoder interface without errors. |
| `NPVS‑REQ‑008` | SHALL play live H.264 adaptive streams via DASH and HLS, handling manifest updates and segment fetching, advancing the position at real-time 1x rate with adaptive bitrate switching and a stable pipeline during continuous live playback. |
| `NPVS‑REQ‑009` | SHALL transition playback between the PLAYING and PAUSED states — holding the position stationary during pause and advancing it at 1x rate (±100 ms) during play, with frames rendered only during the PLAYING state — across video codecs and containers (H.264, HEVC including HEVC HDR and the MKV container, VP9 including VP9 HDR, AV1, 4K content in the MKV container, 4K VP9), audio codecs (OPUS, Vorbis, WAV), and audio sample rates (44.1 kHz, 48 kHz, 96 kHz). |
| `NPVS‑REQ‑010` | SHALL transition H.264 playback between the PLAYING and PAUSED states across the 23, 24, 25, 29, 30, 50, 59, and 60 fps frame rates, holding the position stationary during pause and advancing it at 1x rate during play, with the rendered-frame rate consistent with the stream. |
| `NPVS‑REQ‑011` | SHALL transition HEVC playback between the PLAYING and PAUSED states across the 23, 24, 25, 29, 30, 50, 59, and 60 fps frame rates, holding the position stationary during pause and advancing it at 1x rate during play, with the rendered-frame rate consistent with the stream. |
| `NPVS‑REQ‑012` | SHALL transition AV1 playback between the PLAYING and PAUSED states across the 23, 24, 25, 29, 30, 50, 59, and 60 fps frame rates, holding the position stationary during pause and advancing it at 1x rate during play, with the rendered-frame rate consistent with the stream. |
| `NPVS‑REQ‑013` | SHALL transition VP9 playback between the PLAYING and PAUSED states across the 23, 24, 25, 29, 30, 50, 59, and 60 fps frame rates, holding the position stationary during pause and advancing it at 1x rate during play, with the rendered-frame rate consistent with the stream. |
| `NPVS‑REQ‑014` | SHALL maintain a stable play-pause state machine under prolonged playback exceeding 30 seconds and under repeated cycles of three or more consecutive play-pause transitions (AAC, H.264 over DASH and HLS, HEVC including the MKV container, MKV-container video, and VP9), with correct position behaviour and no buffer starvation, resource leaks, or errors. |
| `NPVS‑REQ‑015` | SHALL perform forward fast-forward trick play at 2x rate, advancing the position at the requested rate with a stable pipeline, across H.264 (including 24, 25, 29, and 60 fps and the MOV container), HEVC (including the MKV container), VP9 (including VP9 HDR), AV1, MKV-container video, DASH and HLS adaptive streams, and AAC audio-only content. |
| `NPVS‑REQ‑016` | SHOULD perform forward fast-forward trick play at 3x rate, advancing the position at the requested rate with a stable pipeline, across H.264 (including 24, 25, 29, and 60 fps and the MOV container), HEVC (including the MKV container), VP9 (including VP9 HDR), AV1, MKV-container video, DASH and HLS adaptive streams, and AAC audio-only content. |
| `NPVS‑REQ‑017` | SHOULD perform forward fast-forward trick play at 4x rate, advancing the position at the requested rate with a stable pipeline, across H.264 (including 24, 25, 29, and 60 fps and the MOV container), HEVC (including the MKV container), VP9 (including VP9 HDR), AV1, MKV-container video, DASH and HLS adaptive streams, and AAC audio-only content. |
| `NPVS‑REQ‑018` | SHALL perform slow-motion playback at 0.5x rate, advancing the position at the requested rate (±25%) while maintaining A/V synchronization, across H.264 (including 24, 25, 29, and 60 fps and the MOV container), HEVC (including the MKV container), VP9 (including VP9 HDR), AV1, MKV-container video, and AAC audio-only content. |
| `NPVS‑REQ‑019` | SHALL perform slow-motion playback at 0.75x rate, advancing the position at the requested rate (±25%) while maintaining A/V synchronization, across H.264 (including 24, 25, 29, and 60 fps and the MOV container), HEVC (including the MKV container), VP9 (including VP9 HDR), AV1, MKV-container video, and AAC audio-only content. |
| `NPVS‑REQ‑020` | SHALL perform forward seek both during active playback and while paused, reaching the target position within ±1 second and resuming smooth playback with continuous frame rendering — during active playback across video content (H.264, HEVC including the MKV container, AV1, DASH streams, HLS streams) and audio-only content (OPUS, AAC, and content in the MP3, MP4, WAV, and WebM containers), and while paused across H.264 (including 24, 25, 29, and 60 fps and the MOV container), HEVC, AV1, and VP9 HDR content. |
| `NPVS‑REQ‑021` | SHALL perform backward seek both during active playback and while paused, reaching the target position within ±1 second and resuming smooth playback with continuous frame rendering — during active playback across video content (H.264, HEVC including the MKV container, AV1, DASH streams, HLS streams) and audio-only content (OPUS, AAC, and content in the MP3, MP4, WAV, and WebM containers), and while paused across H.264 (including 24, 25, 29, and 60 fps and the MOV container), HEVC, AV1, and VP9 HDR content. |
| `NPVS‑REQ‑022` | SHOULD sustain repeated forward seek operations for AAC, H.264 over DASH, and HLS content, reaching each target position correctly with continuous frame rendering and no errors across all iterations. |
| `NPVS‑REQ‑023` | SHOULD sustain repeated backward seek operations for AAC, H.264 over DASH, and HLS content, reaching each target position correctly with continuous frame rendering and no errors across all iterations. |
| `NPVS‑REQ‑024` | SHALL support seeking towards end-of-stream both during active playback and while paused for DASH streams, HLS streams, and content in the MKV, MP4, and WebM containers, reaching the near-EOS target within ±1 second and continuing until end-of-stream is detected with correct PTS monotonicity. |
| `NPVS‑REQ‑025` | SHALL perform combined forward-seek plus 2x fast-forward operations for H.264 (including 24, 25, 29, and 60 fps), AAC, DASH streams, and HLS streams, correctly positioning playback and sustaining the accelerated rate with continuous frame rendering. |
| `NPVS‑REQ‑026` | SHALL perform combined backward-seek plus 2x fast-forward operations for H.264 (including 24, 25, 29, and 60 fps), AAC, DASH streams, and HLS streams, correctly positioning playback and sustaining the accelerated rate with continuous frame rendering. |
| `NPVS‑REQ‑027` | SHALL detect end-of-stream at the natural end of the stream followed by correct pipeline cleanup, for audio-only content (AAC, AC3, EAC3, OPUS, WAV) and for DASH and HLS adaptive streams. |
| `NPVS‑REQ‑028` | SHALL query and validate stream duration against the expected baseline within tolerance for video content (content in the MKV container including 4K, 4K VP9, DASH streams, H.264 over the MOV container, HEVC over the MKV container, HLS streams, MP4 container, VP9 HDR) and audio content (MP3, WAV). |
| `NPVS‑REQ‑029` | SHALL maintain video PTS synchronization for H.264, HEVC (including the MKV container), VP9, AV1, and HLS content, with PTS increasing strictly monotonically and progressing in alignment with the expected per-frame duration and the playback position (±250 ms). |
| `NPVS‑REQ‑030` | SHALL monitor frame-rendering performance for H.264, HEVC, VP9, and AV1 content, with the rendered-frame count incrementing at the stream frame rate and the dropped-frame count remaining within the acceptable baseline during 1x playback. |
| `NPVS‑REQ‑031` | SHALL keep the measured first-frame rendering latency below the configured threshold (default 100 ms) during regular playback for H.264, HEVC, VP9, DASH streams, HLS streams, and MKV-container content. |
| `NPVS‑REQ‑032` | SHALL keep the measured latency of trick-play operations below the configured threshold for 2x fast-forward (AAC, AC3, EAC3) and for forward and backward seek (H.264, HEVC). |
| `NPVS‑REQ‑033` | SHALL switch between audio tracks of the same codec (AAC and EAC3) within a multi-track stream both during active playback and while paused, resuming with the selected track without interrupting video playback and with correct position synchronization. |
| `NPVS‑REQ‑034` | SHALL switch between audio streams of different codecs during active playback and while paused — AAC↔EAC3, AC3↔AAC, AC3↔EAC3, OPUS↔AAC, OPUS↔AC3, OPUS↔EAC3, OPUS↔Vorbis, and Dolby Digital Plus 5.1↔HE-AAC — confirming the codec change while maintaining video playback continuity. |
| `NPVS‑REQ‑035` | SHALL mute audio playback by setting the volume level to 0.0 for AAC, EAC3, and OPUS content during continuous playback, keeping the player and audio-output volume levels synchronized and maintaining a stable pipeline. |
| `NPVS‑REQ‑036` | SHALL apply low-volume audio levels for AAC, EAC3, and OPUS content during continuous playback, keeping the player and audio-output volume levels synchronized with precise control and maintaining a stable pipeline. |
| `NPVS‑REQ‑037` | SHOULD sustain repeated audio volume changes for AAC, EAC3, and OPUS content during continuous playback, keeping the player and audio-output volume levels synchronized across all iterations and maintaining a stable pipeline. |
| `NPVS‑REQ‑038` | SHALL detect depletion of the video buffer, with a pipeline constructed using a custom `appsrc` push-mode fed with a limited number of video buffers, once the pushed buffers are exhausted and emit the video buffer-underflow signal from the video output, verifying the signal is received correctly, across H.264, HEVC, VP9, AV1, 4K content in the MKV container, and 4K VP9. |
| `NPVS‑REQ‑039` | SHALL detect depletion of the audio buffer, with a pipeline constructed using a custom `appsrc` push-mode fed with a limited number of audio buffers, once the pushed buffers are exhausted and emit the audio buffer-underflow signal from the audio output, verifying the signal is received correctly, across AAC, EAC3, OPUS, MP3, and MKV-container audio. |
| `NPVS‑REQ‑040` | SHALL recover video playback, with a pipeline constructed using a custom `appsrc` push-mode, after a video buffer-underflow signal by pushing additional buffers into the pipeline and continuing playback until end-of-stream, across H.264, HEVC, VP9, AV1, 4K content in the MKV container, and 4K VP9. |
| `NPVS‑REQ‑041` | SHALL recover audio playback, with a pipeline constructed using a custom `appsrc` push-mode, after an audio buffer-underflow signal by pushing additional buffers into the pipeline and continuing playback until end-of-stream, across AAC, EAC3, OPUS, MP3, and MKV-container audio. |
| `NPVS‑REQ‑042` | SHALL emit the video buffer-underflow signal from the video output when the video track is exhausted while audio content remains available — for a stream containing a shorter video track and a longer audio track — verifying the signal is received at the point the video content ends. |
| `NPVS‑REQ‑043` | SHALL emit the audio buffer-underflow signal from the audio output when the audio track is exhausted while video content remains available — for a stream containing a shorter audio track and a longer video track — verifying the signal is received at the point the audio content ends. |
| `NPVS‑REQ‑044` | SHALL continue video playback without interruption when the audio track is exhausted, verifying that video continues to render after the audio content ends. |
| `NPVS‑REQ‑045` | SHALL continue audio playback without interruption when the video track is exhausted, verifying that audio continues to render after the video content ends. |
| `NPVS‑REQ‑046` | SHALL recover video playback after a video buffer-underflow signal by seeking to a position where video content is available and confirming video resumes rendering from that position, for a stream whose video track ends before its audio track. |
| `NPVS‑REQ‑047` | SHALL recover audio playback after an audio buffer-underflow signal by seeking to a position where audio content is available and confirming audio resumes rendering from that position, for a stream whose audio track ends before its video track. |
| `NPVS‑REQ‑048` | SHALL perform adaptive bitrate upgrade and downgrade during H.264 and HEVC DASH playback, changing video resolution seamlessly at each transition (verified via the reported video dimensions) without pipeline interruption or A/V synchronization loss. |
| `NPVS‑REQ‑049` | SHALL play H.264 and HEVC video at fixed low, standard, and high bitrate tiers with the corresponding connection speed and resolution, maintaining frame-rendering performance and A/V synchronization with no frame drops or stalls. |
| `NPVS‑REQ‑050` | MAY detect and handle dynamic resolution switching in the ascending (upward) direction, stepping through each of the stream's resolutions from 144p up to 2160p, for H.264, HEVC, VP9, and AV1 content, transitioning through each higher resolution (verified via the reported video dimensions) without decoding failures, errors, or frame drops. |
| `NPVS‑REQ‑051` | MAY detect and handle dynamic resolution switching in the descending (downward) direction, stepping through each of the stream's resolutions from 2160p down to 144p, for H.264, HEVC, VP9, and AV1 content, transitioning through each lower resolution (verified via the reported video dimensions) without decoding failures, errors, or frame drops. |
| `NPVS‑REQ‑052` | SHALL perform channel changes without pipeline interruption or rendering degradation — 4K HLS ↔ H.264 DASH, AV1 ↔ HEVC, DASH ↔ HLS, HD ↔ SD, and OPUS ↔ AAC audio — enabling successful playback of the second stream in each case. |
